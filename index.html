Takeaways from IS201

(This was important for me, as I'll be applying for IS)

Basics in Business

Never having taken any business-related classes prior, it was a lot for me to take in initially! It was interesting to see how organizations and companies function internally, and how I will be able to contribute to them once I attain my IS degree!

Databases, VBA, WebDev

With my 1 year of experience with Computer Science, these units of the class were the easiest for me! It was nice getting a refresher of basic programming principles through the quizzes and projects in class.

Statistical Analysis

I've taken multiple stats class prior, so none of the concepts explored in these units were new to me. However, it was a helpful experience simulating how I would be using data and statistics in a workplace setting.

Bodybuilding

Lifting Weights

Growing up overweight, I've always had struggled with my physical health. Around 11th grade, I finally came to and decided that I eat less and take my health more seriously. Unfortunately, I took it too far and I ended up being severely underweight. The summer after 12th grade, I decided to enroll to a local gym and thus began my bodybuilding journey. I'm not massive by any means, but I have come a long way from where I began. Nowadays I hit the gym almost daily, and I take my diet very seriously. The gym has become my safe space, and I treat working out as my meditation!

Volleyball

Favorite Team-Sport

I've always been in love with sports despite my previous unathleticism. Volleyball has always struck out to me not only because I love team-sports, but also because it is also a sport that requires a high level of coordination. I used to participate in my highschool's volleyball summer camp, and although we didn't have enough men to make up a team, I would have loved to be part of that. Not having played in a long time due to physical and medical circumstances, I am very rusty, however, I did train to be a setter during those aforementioned summer camps. I'd love to get back into playing soon now that I'm in BYU!

Biking

Fun on two wheels

Ironically enough, I only learned how to ride a bike around Winter 2024. I never had the chance to learn because my family was unable to afford a bike when I was young. Once I moved in with my step-family, I asked if I could use one of their bikes to learn, and so I did. Now it's one of my favorite past-times. To me it's more than getting from point A to point B, I feel free and at peace whenever I'm on my bike. I've always wanted to try Mountain Biking, so that's on my bucket list while I'm here in Provo!

Indoor Bouldering

The Thrill Without the Risk

My most recent hobby thus far! A good friend of mine introduced me to indoor bouldering on a whim, and I agreed to go just to try it out. I've only gone three times at the time of writing this, but I'm definitely hooked! I love the idea of puzzling out a boulder and having to use my whole body in ways I never have before to finish climbs. Moreover, since it's indoors, there are no risks of falling to my imminent death by the hands of sharp rocks, so that's also a plus!
